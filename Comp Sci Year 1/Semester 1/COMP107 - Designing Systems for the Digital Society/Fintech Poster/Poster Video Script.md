Hi everyone, I'm Arthur Jameson, and I'm here to talk about my poster on the fintech giant, Klarna. 

Klarna is an industry leader in the Buy Now Pay Later Market and was one of the pioneers in the space. Klarna offers users the ability to defer payment and/or pay in multiple instalments.

 For consumers, Klarna offers increased flexibility when purchasing things online, or in store. 
 For merchants it offers a promise of increased performance. 
 It has helped some companies increase their conversion rates by 40%. Including various ecommerce site such as "wish.com", "Adlibris", "fitnessguru".

Klarna's strengths include: 
- Increased flexibility for customers when shopping.
- Higher Risk Protections through their "pay on delivery" service increasing confidence for consumers shopping online.
- Trust is hugely important in fintech and so Klarna's trusted brand name is probably its biggest advantage, and as a pioneer in this space Klarna is already trusted by millions. Merchants and consumers alike.

Klarna does have weaknesses such as:
- A vulnerability to payment defaults. Since Klarna is lending money, and since it assumes more risk on behalf of its merchant and consumer customers, the company is put in significant jeopardy if large amounts of consumers default on their payments.
- There is also a concern around the potential misuse of Klarna's services by consumers. 
- Since many people don't understand fully the way in which using these services can affect them, it makes is plausible that desperate or financially illiterate people to misuse their services.

- That, while a weakness is also one of the many ethical considerations around Klarna. 
- The ability to defer payments with Klarna could encourage overspending from vulnerable people, especially young people. Klarna has been previously criticised for no-risk wording in its marketing.

- This with combined with late fees and high interest rates on finance payments, could cause some people to accumulate debt that they then struggle to pay off.

- Its vitally important that Klarna and companies like it have transparent terms to help alleviate the effects of some of these issues.

- Klarna could improve its services through Increased transparency around the way that your use of Klarna affects your credit score, possibly even a Klarna credit tracker.
- While Klarna is already a global company, some markets are still untapped, such as India.
- Expansion into these markets, could increase it value to its merchant customers, as it could promise even larger performance boosts to do increased user base.
- Diversifying the services offered by Klarna could also increase its utility. Such as adding an AI spending advisor or more advanced budgeting tools.

Klarna does face a few threats:
-  the first being market saturation. The BNPL market is continuously growing, it grew more than 10% in 2024, and while Klarna is still the industry leader with 35% market share, it must continue to innovate to stay ahead of competitors such as Affirm and Clearpay.
- Klarna has also faced regulatory challenges before. This once resulted in a $50 million fine for violating anti-money laundering rules. Klarna must strictly follow regulations, especially while expanding, or else it may face more fines or even bans in certain countries.
