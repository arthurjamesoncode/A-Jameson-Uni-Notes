## What is AI?
AI, or artificial intelligence, is a field of study in computer science that has been growing for the last 60 or so years. Exactly what it is depends on who you ask.

- "The goal of work in AI is to build machines that perform tasks normally requiring human intelligence." - Nilsson, 1971
- "AI is everything we can’t do with today’s computers" - Possibly McCarthy, 1980s
- "My definition of AI is any algorithm that is new in computer science. Once the algorithm becomes accepted then it’s not AI, it’s just a boring algorithm. At one time windows, mouse, menus, scroll bars etc. were considered an AI technique for making computers understand natural language" - Possibly Keane, 1990s
- "A branch of computer science dealing with the simulation of intelligent behaviour in computers. The capability of a machine to imitate intelligent human behaviour" - Merriam Webster Dictionary

AI has been applied in many different areas:
- Natural language processing: understanding, generating, translating.
- Vision: object recognition, face recognition, scene recognition.
- Robotics: car driving, autonomous aerial vehicles.
- Theorem Proving: four colour theorem.
- Speech recognition: smartphones.
- Game playing: chess, GO, poker.
- Semantic web search: structured search.
- Diagnosis in healthcare.

AI, as it should be understood in this module is two things:
- The **science** of understanding intelligent entities: developing theories/models which attempt to explain and predict the nature of such entities.
- The **engineering** of intelligent entities.

## Types of AI
There are two main types of AI: 
- AGI, artificial general intelligence, also called strong AI
- Narrow AI, also called weak AI.

An AGI is an artificial system that can accomplish any intellectual task that humans can perform. Often sentience or consciousness are also required definitions of AGI. This is not a "real" thing in that it as of yet does not exist. This is still the realm of science fiction.

Narrow AI is an artificial system that is able to solve a small number of specific intellectual problems. Narrow AI does not experience consciousness. Examples of this would be chess engines, poker solvers, computer vision etc.

Before ChatGPT and other LLMs, and more generally generative AI, were created in 2022/2023, almost all AI researchers and practitioners would have described their goal as narrow AI. This has changed a bit since generative AI, while still technically Narrow AI, is a huge step towards AGI.
## Four Views of AI
There are 4 main views of what we should be trying to achieve with AI:
- Systems that **think** like **humans**
- Systems that **act** like **humans**
- Systems that **think rationally** (think in a correct way)
- Systems that **act rationally** (act optimally to achieve a goal)

### Thinking Humanly VS Acting Humanly
One way of thinking about AI is that we should be attempting to create a machine that simulates human thought.

Another school of thought surrounding this, is that we should be building machines that simulate the intelligent behaviour of humans. This school of thought argues that it is not necessary nor desirable that intelligent machines process information in the same way as we do, but the goal is to create machines that are able to behave in a human way.

This emphasis on simulating behaviour rather than internal processes has consequences for the question: "When can we count a machine as being intelligent?". Essentially the question changes from "Can machines think?" to "Can machines behave intelligently?"
### Thinking VS Acting Rationally
While investigating how we **actually** think and act is one route to AI, another is investigating how we **should** think and act.

To do this we can use techniques from logic an probability theory to investigate and create machines that can reason correctly. The result from this is an idealised reasoning.

Acting rationally refers to acting to achieve your goals given, your beliefs. We can use techniques from economics and game theory to investigate and create machines that act rationally.

>A current problem of generative AI is that it does not reason correctly.
